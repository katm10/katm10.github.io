Me > Meat (Resilience Challenge)

January - March 2016

From January until March,  I participated in the Resilience Challenge in San Francisco.  If you are interested, you can learn more about it  at their homepage for participantsor at their public website.In this challenge, we had to come up with a solution to this problem: How can we reduce our carbon emissions while increasing community resilience in the Bay Area? To solve this, my team came up with the idea for Me > Meat. Me > Meat is an app that allows you to see how much carbon dioxide, methane, and water is used in the process of harvesting different meat products by scanning the barcode. Once you do this, other, more eco-friendly products will also appear with recipes for each of them in the hope that we can decrease meat consumption and thus decrease carbon dioxide emissions. Although we weren't able to complete the app, I will try to program it over the summer, so keep an eye out for further notice! ☺ You can view the first prototype hereor our second prototype here. In this challenge, I learned how to work with a team of people I had never met, organize and manage my team, use human-based design, and prototype.

Udacity Girls Who Code 
Hack-a-Thon

March 17, 2016

On March 17, I attended a Girls Who Code Hack-a-Thon over at Udacity!It was an amazing experience, fo I got to meet the people who created much of the Udacity content that I love! During this 2-hour hack-a-thon, we were put into teams with other coders whom we had never met, and we got the task of developing a way to make more girls interested in coding. As a team, we all shared an idea we had and voted to determine what we would do. I wanted to make a mural that incorporated images of amazing women in tech and could be used as a "selfie wall", but my idea didn't get enough votes. (If anyone wants to make this concept a reality, contact me using the info below.) Out final idea was to make a Youtube channel that incorporated different videos about the technology field, and although we lost, it was a wonderful experience! You can read about the winning team at the Girls Who Code blog.
