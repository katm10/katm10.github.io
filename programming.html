Sharon 

June 24-25, 2016

I entered a B-Stem Project hack-a-thon with my program, Sharon. You can learn more about our project on  Devpost or on our website prototype, but the TL;DR is that Sharon is an automated SMS messaging robot that allows users within the same zipcode to discuss important topics in their community in a safe and anonymous way.  I worked on this project with two other high schoolers, who focused on the website and marketing. While they did that, I wrote the program in C# on Visual Studios by using the documentation for Outlook add-ins. I was new to all of these, but it was surprisingly easy. Despite the differences, much of my knowledge about Java applied to C#, which was very helpful. After working on this for two days, we submitted our project, and it won! Thanks to B-Stem and Microsoft for this hack-a-thon.

Me > Meat (Resilience Challenge)

January - March 2016

From January until March,  I participated in the Resilience Challenge in San Francisco.  If you are interested, you can learn more about it at their public website. You can also view this article about the Resilience Challenge I participated in.In this challenge, we had to come up with a solution to this problem: How can we reduce our carbon emissions while increasing community resilience in the Bay Area? To solve this, my team came up with the idea for Me > Meat. Me > Meat is an app that allows you to see how much carbon dioxide, methane, and water is used in the process of harvesting different meat products by scanning the barcode. Once you do this, other, more eco-friendly products will also appear with recipes for each of them in the hope that we can decrease meat consumption and thus decrease carbon dioxide emissions. Although we weren't able to complete the app, I will try to program it over the summer, so keep an eye out for further notice! ☺ You can view the first prototype hereor our second prototype here. In this challenge, I learned how to work with a team of people I had never met, organize and manage my team, use human-based design, and prototype. 

Udacity Girls Who Code 
Hack-a-Thon

March 17, 2016

On March 17, I attended a Girls Who Code Hack-a-Thon over at Udacity!It was an amazing experience, fo I got to meet the people who created much of the Udacity content that I love! During this 2-hour hack-a-thon, we were put into teams with other coders whom we had never met, and we got the task of developing a way to make more girls interested in coding. As a team, we all shared an idea we had and voted to determine what we would do. I wanted to make a mural that incorporated images of amazing women in tech and could be used as a "selfie wall", but my idea didn't get enough votes. (If anyone wants to make this concept a reality, contact me using the info below.) Out final idea was to make a Youtube channel that incorporated different videos about the technology field, and although we lost, it was a wonderful experience! You can read about the winning team at the Girls Who Code blog.
