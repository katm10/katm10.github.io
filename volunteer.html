Volunteering Blog Updated 6/5/16

Letter Writers

Every last Sunday of the month, I work with about ten other volunteers to write letters to anyone in our community who is going through hard times, such as people who are going through surgeries, who have lost a close friend or relative, who are going through a big change in their life, or who simply need kind words of support. Actively writing out each letter by hand is a nice way to show those in need that someone cares about them and is there for them. 

Brown Bag Sunday

Every first Sunday of the month, I work with other volunteers to make brown bag lunches for the less fortunate in the area. We make 250 bags every month which are then distributed to the poor and homeless. I have participated in this almost every month since fifth grade, and it is a nice experience, for I learned to be more grateful and give back to the community.

Winter Wonderland

December 2015

As part of the Interact Club at BHS, I helped set up and participate in our annual Winter Wonderland. Winter Wonderland is a comunity event for children in the area. Anyone may come and enjoy the event for free, and we have many fun activities, like face painting, cookie decorating, coloring, and more! Children may play a life-sized Candyland game as they journey from each activity to the next. I helped organize and run Winter Wonderland and we had a great turnout!

Bayfront Clean Up 

Every fall, Burlingame seeks volunteers to pick up litter and trash from the bayside to help keep our environment clean and healthy! The litter that is left near the water eventually falls in and is swept out to the bay and then to the Pacific Ocean, where it can harm the wildlife. The bayfront clean up is a great way to help out the precious SF wetland ecosystem and do our part to protect the wildlife by removing our trash and pollutants.
